Robert Neese Neyland

Brigadier General. College Football Coach. Father of the Seven Maxims of Football.
Awarded the Distinguished Service Medal and Legion of Merit. Also became a member of the Order of the British Empire for his Military service.
General Neyland was also considered one of the best Defensive college football coaches ever!

    Here's a time line of General Neyland's life:

  • 1892 - Born in Greenville, Texas.
  • 1913 - Appointed to West Point in New York.
  • 1916 - Graduated West Point and commissioned as an officer in the Corps of Engineers. Served in France during World War I and returned and earned an Engineering Degree from Massachusetts Institute of Technology.
  • 1919 - Army football assistant coach.
  • 1923 - Married Ada "Peggy" Fitch from Grand Rapids, Michigan
  • 1925 - Tennessee football assistant coach.
  • 1926-1934 - Tennessee football Head coach.
  • 1936-1940 - Tennessee football Head coach.
  • 1941 - Recalled to Military service again and served in the China-Burma-India Theater during World War II.
  • 1946 - Retired from the Army as a Brigadier General and was awarded the Distinguished Servie Medal, Legion of Merit, and was made a member of the Order of the British Empire.
  • 1946-1952 - Tennessee football Head coach.
  • 1956 - Inducted into the College Football Hall of Fame.
  • 1962 - Dies at the age of 70 and the Robert R. Neyland Scholarship Fund was establised by Andrew D. Holt, President of the Unversity of Tennessee.
